‚è±Ô∏è 3 minutes

Bumble's board is considering three strategic paths. Select ONE option that you will develop and defend. Each path requires Bumble to make difficult trade-offs ‚Äî remember, strategy is about choosing what not to do.

üß†
AI-Powered Smart Matchmaking
Reposition as "the world's smartest matchmaker" ‚Äî using insights from leading couples therapists and psychologists to create deeper compatibility matching. Completely abandon the women-first positioning to compete directly with Hinge on matching quality.
Key trade-off: Give up the distinctive women-first brand to compete head-to-head with Hinge's AI capabilities.
What this would mean: Bumble would invest heavily in AI R&D, partner with relationship experts, and relaunch as a premium matching service ‚Äî no longer defined by who messages first.
üéâ
In-Person Events & Community
Pivot from app-based dating to organising real-world singles events and community groups. Help people "get off the app and into the world." Become a connection company that happens to have an app.
Key trade-off: Events require physical infrastructure in every city ‚Äî fundamentally different economics from a scalable app business.
What this would mean: Bumble would build out event operations globally, acquire community platforms, and create spaces for singles to meet face-to-face.
üõ°Ô∏è
Safety & Trust Leadership
Rebuild differentiation around being the safest, most trustworthy dating platform. Lead industry standards for verification, AI moderation, and protection ‚Äî especially for women.
Key trade-off: Safety features are increasingly being copied by competitors. Can Bumble stay ahead in a "safety arms race"?
What this would mean: Bumble would invest in cutting-edge verification technology, stricter moderation, and position safety as its core brand promise.
